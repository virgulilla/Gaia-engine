# PG-003 — Player Role

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Approved

**Owner:** Gaia Engine Team

**Last Updated:** 2026-06-27

---

# Purpose

Defines the role of the player inside Project Gaia.

The player's role determines how they interact with the simulated world without violating the integrity of the ecosystem.

---

# Scope

This specification defines:

- player identity
- player capabilities
- player limitations
- interaction philosophy

It does not define:

- UI
- progression
- gameplay systems
- engine implementation

---

# Philosophy

The player is an observer.

The player is not an organism.

The player is not a deity.

The player influences nature without controlling it.

---

# Player Identity

The player represents an external scientific observer.

The world exists independently of the player.

Life neither knows nor depends on the player's presence.

---

# Primary Objectives

The player seeks to:

- understand ecosystems
- document discoveries
- observe evolution
- perform experiments
- preserve knowledge

There is no conquest objective.

There is no victory condition.

---

# Player Capabilities

The player may:

- move freely through the world
- pause the simulation
- change simulation speed
- inspect organisms
- inspect species
- inspect environments
- record discoveries
- create experiments

---

# Player Limitations

The player may never:

- directly control organisms
- force organism behaviour
- override biological rules
- bypass simulation systems
- alter engine execution

All influence must pass through Gaia Engine systems.

---

# Observation

Observation is the player's primary interaction.

Players are encouraged to:

- watch behaviour
- identify patterns
- compare ecosystems
- predict outcomes

Observation should be rewarding.

---

# Experimentation

Players may perform controlled interventions.

Examples include:

- introducing new species
- modifying climate
- changing terrain
- adjusting resources

Every intervention produces natural consequences.

---

# Knowledge

Knowledge belongs to the player.

Simulation state belongs to Gaia Engine.

The player's knowledge persists independently from any individual world.

---

# Ethical Design

Project Gaia avoids encouraging unnecessary destruction.

Players are rewarded for understanding ecosystems rather than exploiting them.

---

# Relationship With Gaia Engine

Gaia Engine owns the simulation.

The player interacts with the simulation exclusively through Project Gaia systems.

No player action bypasses Gaia Engine.
